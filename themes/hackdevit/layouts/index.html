About HACK DEVit

It is projected that 68% of the world population will live in urban areas by 2050 [1]. The importance of improving cities is becoming increasingly apparent.

Furthermore, living in a city feels like subscribing for services. Rent, electricity, water, heat, internet; these are all pay-per-month services. Streets, schools, hospitals, infrastructure in general; these may not look like services but citizens expect all those to function perfectly because they pay taxes.

This system works better in some cities than others, but let's do an experiment: Instead of `subscribing to services` let's think of living in a city as `fixing for others`. `Fixing` as in things break and maybe people break them, but blaming doesn't help, fixing does. `For others`, as in "other people that live in the same city as us”. Let’s assume the most uncomfortable instance: we don't know these people; they probably disagree with us; they may not even like what we build for them, even though we do it to help. But that’s ok: the process of society improvement is messy, yet efficient.

So, here's this hackathon's theme: Urban improvement with the mindset of fixing for others.
